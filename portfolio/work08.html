Elysium Case Study

made with adobe xd, photoshop, and procreate

timeline: design, 48 hours

Brief

I wanted to create a mobile music/rhythm gaming interface. The interface itself was inspired by the aesthetics of the video games Gris and Journey: both games involved internal reflection being reflected by a physical journey, and I wanted the replicate that sort of feeling through this interface.

Idle Animations

I tried to make the home screen/menu screens have animations that played with perspective while not being overwhelmingly busy. Added to make the interface less flat and more playful to look at.

Button Animations

Menu interface is shaped like a "hand" to mimic the hand motif going through the home/menu screens. The palette of the game is also reflected in this interface while also hinting about the potential worlds to unlock: desert (red), sky (blue), forest (green), and mountain (yellow-brown).

I made it so that the button animations feel like they were "bleeding" like watercolor does. Couldn't implement into Adobe XD, but I wanted this animation to occur once the button is tapped before transitioning to next screen.

Song Selection/Animations

I wanted this prototype to reflect a general idea of how I would want the selection process to look like. I also wanted the environment when playing the rhythm game to match whatever "world" is also being played. Hypothetically, once more worlds are unlocked, different backgrounds for th rhythm part of the game is unlocked. The palette for this interface would also change.

Story Interface

Story is told in correspondence with when a song is "completed"- more story is unlocked, the more songs you unlock. Updated with each new song completion.

Achievement Interface

The design of these achievements aimed to be simple and related to purpose of the achievement itself: I also created a "hint" achievement to help guide the user into knowing what they could potentially do next to get another achievement.

Prototype
Interactable version.
Reflection

Learned more about the potential animations that could be done as well as the potential limitations of trying to do a more complicated animation process in Adobe XD.
